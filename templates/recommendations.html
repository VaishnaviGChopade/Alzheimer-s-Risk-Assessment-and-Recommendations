  • 0: No alcohol consumption
  • 1 - 7: Light drinking (up to 1 unit per day)

  • 15 - 20: Heavy drinking

Excessive alcohol consumption is linked to cognitive decline and an increased risk of Alzheimer's.

Range: 0 to 10
  • 0: No physical activity
  • 1 - 3: Very low (minimal movement or occasional activity)
  • 4 - 6: Moderate (light to moderate activity a few times per week)
  • 7 - 9: High (regular exercise most days of the week)
  • 10: Very high (daily, intense or athlete-level activity)

Regular physical activity supports brain health, improves memory, and reduces the risk of Alzheimer's disease.

Range: 0 to 10
  • 0 - 3: Poor diet quality (high in unhealthy foods, low in fruits and vegetables)
  • 4 - 6: Average diet quality (moderate balance of healthy and unhealthy foods)
  • 7 - 10: High diet quality (rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins)

A balanced diet with proper nutrition supports brain health and helps reduce the risk of cognitive decline.

Range: 4 to 10
  • 4 - 5: Poor sleep quality (difficulty falling asleep, frequent waking, feeling tired in the morning)
  • 6 - 7: Average sleep quality (occasional disturbances, generally restful)
  • 8 - 10: Good sleep quality (consistent, uninterrupted, and restful sleep)

Quality sleep is essential for brain function, memory consolidation, and reducing Alzheimer's risk.

Range: 60 to 120 mmHg
  • 60 - 80: Normal
  • 81 - 89: Elevated

Elevated diastolic pressure can increase the risk of vascular damage and has been correlated with impaired brain function and memory issues in later life.

Range: 150 to 300 mg/dL
  • 150 - 199: Desirable
  • 200 - 239: Borderline High
  • 240 - 300: High

High total cholesterol levels are associated with an increased risk of cardiovascular diseases and cognitive impairment, which may contribute to Alzheimer's progression.

Range: 50 to 200 mg/dL.
  • 50-100 mg/dL: Optimal
  • 101-129 mg/dL: Near or above optimal
  • 130-159 mg/dL: Borderline high
  • 160-200 mg/dL: High to very high

Range: 20 to 100 mg/dL
  • 20 - 39 mg/dL: Low HDL cholesterol level (associated with a higher risk of heart disease and cognitive decline)
  • 40 - 60 mg/dL: Optimal HDL cholesterol level (helps remove bad cholesterol and reduce Alzheimer's risk)
  • 61 - 100 mg/dL: High HDL cholesterol level (associated with lower risk of heart disease and better brain health)

HDL cholesterol is often referred to as "good cholesterol" because it helps remove excess LDL ("bad" cholesterol) from the bloodstream, reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases and potentially lowering Alzheimer's risk.

Range: 50 to 400 mg/dL

  • 151 - 199 mg/dL: Borderline high triglyceride level (increased risk of heart disease and metabolic issues)
  • 200 - 499 mg/dL: High triglyceride level (associated with a greater risk of cognitive decline and cardiovascular diseases)
  • 500> mg/dL: Very high triglyceride level (severe risk for metabolic syndrome, heart disease, and possible impact on brain health)

Triglycerides are a type of fat found in the blood. Elevated triglyceride levels have been linked to an increased risk of Alzheimer's disease and other cognitive impairments.
